🔧 v0 shortcuts ledger (crude on purpose — the memo indicts these later):
• Team strength = season-to-date runs scored/allowed per game with a 20-game league-average ballast; no schedule adjustment, no recency weighting, no roster awareness.
• Starter adjustment = season RUNS-allowed rate per 9 (not ERA, not FIP), regressed with 60 IP of league average — small samples lie, so the prior is deliberately heavy. Relief innings a listed starter threw count the same as starts.
• Starter owns 57% of the defensive game; the remaining 43% is proxied by the TEAM'S overall runs-allowed rate, which itself still contains rotation innings — the bullpen is not separated out in v0.
• No probable starter listed = team rate used unadjusted, starters recorded as TBD (the lean says so).
• Home advantage = fixed multipliers (1.033/0.967) on expected runs, one number for all 30 parks; NOT fitted.
• Win prob = game-level Pythagenpat on expected runs (x = RPG^0.287); totals distribution = independent Poisson per team — real run distributions are lumpier (extra-inning rules, bullpen chaining), so tail probabilities are modestly overconfident toward the mean.
• Park factors OUT (dropped first per sequencing rule); weather OUT (scope); lineups, platoon splits, umpires, travel/rest OUT (v0 cannot see them).
• Cross-matchup runs use the odds-ratio-free scaling exp = off_rate * def_rate / league_rate — the standard first-order approximation, unfitted.
🙈 What v0 cannot see: bullpen quality and availability (the ~43% of defensive innings it proxies with a team-wide rate), platoon and handedness splits, actual lineups (rest days, injuries, call-ups), park factors, weather (wind alone can swing a total by a run), umpire tendencies, travel/rest, catcher framing, and anything Statcast measures. Its ratings are season-long averages with no recency weighting, its starter view is one regressed runs-allowed number, and its totals distribution is a Poisson convenience. Treat every lean as a diagnostic reading from a deliberately blunt instrument — that is the entire point of the shadow.
